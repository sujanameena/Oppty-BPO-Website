Recruitment Process

The back office is responsible for the recruitment process, from posting job openings to screening resumes to interviewing candidates. The back office also helps to onboard new employees, providing them with the information and resources they need to be successful in their new roles.

Onboarding Employees

Onboarding is the process of integrating new employees into a company’s culture and providing them with the knowledge and skills they need to be successful. The back office plays a key role in onboarding, providing new employees with access to company policies and procedures, training materials, and mentors.

Payroll Management

Payroll management is the process of calculating and distributing employee wages and salaries. The back office is responsible for ensuring that payroll is accurate and timely, and that employees are paid in accordance with company policies and government regulations.

Time and Expense Management

Time and expense management is the process of tracking employee time and expenses. The back office is responsible for ensuring that employees are accurately tracking their time and expenses, and that they are reimbursed for approved expenses in a timely manner.

Payroll Tax

Payroll tax is the tax that employers are required to withhold from employee wages and salaries. The back office is responsible for calculating and submitting payroll tax payments to the government.

Employee Data Management

Employee data management is the process of collecting, storing, and protecting employee data. The back office is responsible for ensuring that employee data is accurate, secure, and accessible only to authorized personnel.

Why Outsource Back Office HR & Payroll
